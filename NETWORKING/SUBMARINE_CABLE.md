# 🌊 Submarine Cable

A submarine cable (also called an undersea cable) is a fiber optic cable laid on the seabed that carries internet and telecommunication data between countries and continents.

More than 95% of international internet traffic is transmitted through submarine fiber optic cables rather than satellites.

---

## 🌍 How It Works

1. Data is converted into light signals.
2. The signals travel through submarine fiber optic cables.
3. Optical repeaters strengthen the signal over long distances.
4. The data reaches a landing station in another country and is routed to its destination.

### Flow

User → ISP → Landing Station → Submarine Cable → Landing Station → Destination ISP → Server

---

## 📍 Landing Station

A landing station is a facility where a submarine cable connects to a country's terrestrial network.

---

## 🚀 Advantages

- High-speed data transmission
- Low latency
- Massive bandwidth capacity
- Reliable international communication
- More cost-effective than satellites

---

## ⚠️ Disadvantages

- Cable damage can disrupt internet connectivity
- Repairs are expensive and time-consuming
- Vulnerable to natural disasters and ship anchors

---

## 🇧🇩 Bangladesh

Bangladesh is connected to the global internet through submarine cable systems, including:

- SEA-ME-WE-4
- SEA-ME-WE-5

These cables provide international internet connectivity for the country.
